Cellular Automata are simulations based on a model that consists of a regular grid of cells, each in one of a finite number of states (such as on and off). Simulations start with all cells in an initial state and are run by updating each cell based on a set of fixed rules described in terms of the cell's current state and the states of its immediate neighbors.

All of these are simulations based on a CA models of a real system, built to better understand it, to observe and predict its behavior, or to perform experiments on the model.
